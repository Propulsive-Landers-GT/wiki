# Google Drive Access

Google Drive is where GTPL stores larger project files that do not belong in GitHub. This usually includes CAD files, simulation files, presentations, reports, images, and other shared team documents. GitHub is mainly for code, scripts, and version controlled documentation. Google Drive is for large files and team files that are easier to organize by folder.

### Getting Access

If you already have a Google account, DM your subteam lead with:

* Your full name
* Your email address
* Your subteam
* What folder or project you need access to

Your subteam lead will either add you directly or forward your email to someone who manages Drive access.

### If You Do Not Have a Google Account

If you do not have a Google account yet:

1. Go to [https://accounts.google.com/signup](https://accounts.google.com/signup)
2. Create an account.
3. Verify your email.
4. DM your subteam lead with the email connected to your Google account.

### What Goes in Google Drive

Use Google Drive for:

* CAD files
* Simulation files
* Test data
* Photos and videos
* Reports and presentations
* Meeting slides
* Shared spreadsheets

Do not use GitHub for large CAD/simulation files unless the repo is specifically set up for that.

### After You Are Added

Once you are added, check your email for a shared folder invitation. You may also need to check the **Shared with me** section in Google Drive.

If you still cannot find the folder, message your subteam lead with:

* The email you want added
* The folder you are trying to access
* A screenshot of what you see

### Good Drive Habits

* Do not move or delete files unless you know what they are.
* Keep files in the correct project folder.
* Use clear file names.
* Do not upload random duplicate copies.
* Ask before reorganizing folders.
* If a file is important, make sure the team owns it and not just your personal account.
